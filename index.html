⊞ Covered-Call Candidates Income from selling calls on names that are in gear — price > 20MA > 50MA on all three of Monthly · Weekly · Daily — and either flat or mildly rising (not parabolic). Calm-but-live ATR and liquidity also scored. Wk = weekly-options eligible · Mo = monthly only
What this finds vs. what it can't see: SCANR screens the underlying — regime, volatility, liquidity — to surface good covered-call candidates. It does not see the option chain (live premium, implied vol, strike open-interest, bid/ask spread). Pick the strike and confirm the premium and spreads are worthwhile in your broker's chain. Selling a call caps upside above the strike, so the ideal name drifts sideways or rises gently — not a runaway mover.

⊞ Coiled / Pre-Breakout Finds names in a tight coil — deep volatility contraction, volume dry-up, range tightening, MA pinch — before the trigger fires. Scored on Daily and Weekly bases. Lean tag shows where pressure sits: ↑ up · ↓ down · ↔ undecided.
What a coil is — and what it isn't: Big moves come out of quiet, not chaos. SCANR measures the coiled condition (contraction + dry-up + pinch) from price/volume. It does NOT predict the move and cannot see what releases the spring — the catalyst, options flow (call skew/IV), short interest, or float aren't in the equity feed. A coil can sit for weeks, deflate, or resolve the "wrong" way. Use this as a watchlist: surface the loaded names, then check the catalyst and options picture in your broker before acting. Direction is genuinely undecided in a coil — the lean tag is pressure, not a forecast.
