READ THIS BEFORE TRUSTING ANY NUMBER BELOW. A backtest shows how this allocation behaved over the chosen window — not what it will return. With only ~4 years of live history for the full sleeve, an expected return drawn from live-ticker mode has a very wide confidence interval. Proxy-extended mode reaches further back but tests analogues of your funds, not the funds themselves. Treat output as "how this strategy would have behaved," never as a forecast. Conversions, IRMAA, and the bridge ladder are deliberately not modeled — they change after-tax terminal wealth, not gross return.

2b · Dual Momentum (rotation overlay)

A separate engine from the breaker above. Each rebalance it ranks the chosen universe by momentum (relative momentum) and holds the top N equally. Before committing, it can check absolute momentum: if a chosen winner's own momentum is below the SGOV/T-bill return, that slot goes to SGOV instead. With the 3/6/12 averaged momentum and the 13-ETF Papa Bear universe, this reproduces the published Muscular Portfolios "Papa Bear" strategy (top 3, no gate) — and lets you race it against variants (your sleeve, an SGOV gate, top 2 vs 3).

Weekly emergency exit (v1.4): keeps the monthly Papa Bear selection, but adds a weekly downside-only safety check. Each week, any held name that closes more than the trigger % below its own N-week MA rotates to SGOV mid-month rather than waiting for month-end. It only ever removes risk between rebalances \u2014 a name re-enters solely at the next monthly rebalance, and only if it is still top-N and back above its MA. This targets the \u201Ca month is a long time when the market is getting slammed\u201D problem without corrupting the medium-term momentum signal with weekly noise on the entry side. Expect: helps in fast sustained selloffs (2008, Mar 2020); can cost a little in sharp dip-then-snap-back weeks (exits, then misses the V-bounce until month-end). The robustness matrix is the referee.
To replicate published Papa Bear: Universe = Papa Bear 13-ETF · Momentum = 3/6/12 average · Hold top 3 · gate = off. The 13-ETF menu carries its own defense (it rotates into gold/Treasuries when stocks fall), so the published version uses no cash gate. On your 5-equity sleeve there is no safe haven, so the SGOV gate is the only defense — that is why the two universes behave differently. "3/6/12 average" ranks on the mean of the trailing 3-, 6-, and 12-month returns, exactly the column on the Papa Bear screen. The Papa Bear menu ignores the sleeve table above and uses its own fixed live/proxy tickers. Note: in Proxy-extended mode the picks show stand-in symbols (IWO\u2248VIOG, EEM\u2248VWO, DBC\u2248PDBC, GLD\u2248IAU, TLT\u2248EDV, etc.) because the real ETFs did not exist in 2007 \u2014 the results panel spells out every substitution.
